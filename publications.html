reverseDAAM: Region-to-Token Interpretability for Diffusion Models

M. A. Waseem, et al.
Under Review · IEEE SMC 2026
We propose reverseDAAM, a post-hoc interpretability framework that maps image regions back to prompt tokens in text-to-image diffusion models. The method aggregates cross-attention scores across timesteps and introduces a Word–BBox Alignment Score to compare models. Validated on Stable Diffusion, SDXL, and DeepFloyd IF-XL, reverseDAAM surfaces systematic alignment failures between what the prompt asks for and where the model paints it.

A Tuning-Fork Network for Improved Building Footprint Extraction

M. A. Waseem, et al.
IEEE IGARSS 2025
TFNet introduces a multi-task segmentation architecture with a Dilated ResNet encoder and dual decoders that jointly predict footprint masks and edge maps. The dual heads act as a "tuning fork" that co-regularizes shape and boundary, achieving 94% F1 on SpaceNet2 and WHU benchmarks with strong generalization across sensors.

M. A. Waseem, et al.
