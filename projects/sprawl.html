§ 01
PROBLEM

What it had to solve.

Urban growth indicators from census data lag by 5–10 years in developing cities. Planners need something more responsive — and globally consistent.

§ 02
APPROACH

How it works.

Landsat imagery from 2005–2020 is segmented frame-by-frame; change detection between adjacent years produces "growth pixels"; a sprawl index aggregates to the municipal level.

§ 03
RESULTS

What it delivered.

Derived sprawl indices correlated with census growth (r = 0.89) while providing annual resolution — five years ahead of the next census.
